# Underlying Competencies (BABOK Chapter 9)

The behaviours, characteristics, knowledge, and personal qualities that make a business analyst effective. These don't usually drive a conversation about *the work* — but they matter for self-assessment, mentoring, role design, hiring, training plans, and explaining "why is this hard."

Each competency has the same internal structure: Purpose, Definition, Effectiveness Measures.

The six competencies, with their sub-areas:

## 9.1 Analytical Thinking and Problem Solving

The cognitive horsepower of the role. Six sub-skills:

- **Creative Thinking** — generating new ideas and approaches
- **Decision Making** — weighing options under uncertainty
- **Learning** — absorbing new domains, tools, contexts quickly
- **Problem Solving** — defining problems clearly and finding workable solutions
- **Systems Thinking** — seeing the whole and the interactions, not just the parts
- **Conceptual Thinking** — abstracting from specifics to patterns
- **Visual Thinking** — using diagrams and models to think and to communicate

**When to surface this**: when a user is stuck on a hard problem; when assessing BA capability; when designing training. The systems-thinking sub-skill in particular is the one most often missing in junior BAs.

## 9.2 Behavioural Characteristics

The "personal qualities" cluster — what makes a BA trustworthy and effective over time:

- **Ethics** — honesty, integrity, fairness
- **Personal Accountability** — owning outcomes, not just activities
- **Trustworthiness** — earning and keeping trust with stakeholders
- **Organization and Time Management** — running their own work
- **Adaptability** — responding to change without flailing

**When to surface this**: hiring conversations; performance reviews; explaining why some BAs are effective despite limited "hard" technical skill.

## 9.3 Business Knowledge

What the BA knows about the business they're analyzing:

- **Business Acumen** — general business literacy (finance, operations, strategy)
- **Industry Knowledge** — the specific industry's structures, regulations, players
- **Organization Knowledge** — the specific company's culture, history, politics
- **Solution Knowledge** — knowledge of the existing or proposed solution
- **Methodology Knowledge** — knowledge of the methods being used (Agile, Lean, etc.)

**When to surface this**: career development conversations; explaining why domain experience matters; deciding when to bring in a domain SME vs. expecting the BA to learn.

## 9.4 Communication Skills

The art of moving information between minds:

- **Verbal Communication** — speaking clearly, adapting to audience
- **Non-Verbal Communication** — body language, tone, energy
- **Written Communication** — documents, emails, models that read clearly
- **Listening** — the most undervalued; active listening, listening for what's not said

**When to surface this**: when communication breakdowns are the apparent problem; when training new BAs; when designing artefacts (the audience needs the right format).

## 9.5 Interaction Skills

The skills of working with and through other people:

- **Facilitation** — running productive group conversations
- **Leadership and Influencing** — shaping outcomes without formal authority
- **Teamwork** — operating well in a team context
- **Negotiation and Conflict Resolution** — getting to workable agreements
- **Teaching** — building capability in others

**When to surface this**: when stakeholder conflict is the apparent problem; when a BA is moving into a more senior or coaching role; when designing workshops (facilitation is the key sub-skill).

## 9.6 Tools and Technology

What the BA uses to do the work:

- **Office Productivity Tools and Technology** — documents, spreadsheets, slides, basic data
- **Business Analysis Tools and Technology** — modelling tools, requirements management tools, BPM platforms, prototyping tools
- **Communication Tools and Technology** — collaboration platforms, video conferencing, persistent chat

**When to surface this**: tool selection conversations; onboarding; understanding why the BA is bottlenecked (often a tool fluency gap).

---

## How to use this reference

Don't pull this into a conversation about *doing the work*. Pull it when the conversation is about:
- Capability assessment ("what skills do I need to develop?")
- Hiring or role design ("what should I look for in a BA?")
- Mentoring or coaching ("how do I help someone grow into this role?")
- Diagnosing chronic friction ("why does this keep being hard?" — sometimes the answer is a competency gap, not a process problem)

For self-assessment, the structure of "Purpose / Definition / Effectiveness Measures" in BABOK gives a useful prompt: for each sub-skill, ask "what does mastery look like in concrete behaviour?"
